FAQ

Study Visa FAQs

Common questions about applying for a student visa abroad.

Do I need IELTS to apply for a study visa?

+

Most English-speaking countries require proof of English proficiency. IELTS is the most widely accepted, but TOEFL, PTE, and Duolingo are also accepted by many institutions. Some universities also offer conditional admission with English language courses.

Can I work while studying abroad?

+

Yes, most countries allow international students to work part-time during studies. Canada allows 20 hours/week during sessions and full-time during breaks. Australia allows 48 hours per fortnight. UK allows 20 hours/week. Specific rules vary by country and visa type.

What is a Statement of Purpose (SOP)?

+

An SOP is a personal essay explaining your academic background, why you chose the specific program and university, your career goals, and why you plan to return to your home country after studies. A well-written SOP significantly improves your visa approval chances. Our team helps craft compelling SOPs.

What happens if my study visa is rejected?

+

A rejection is not the end. We analyze the refusal letter, identify the reasons, address the weaknesses in your application, and help you reapply with a stronger case. Many of our clients have been successfully approved after initial rejections.

How early should I start the study visa process?

+

We recommend starting at least 6‚Äì8 months before your intended course start date. This gives enough time for university applications, receiving offer letters, preparing documents, and the visa processing period. Starting early also gives you more university options.
